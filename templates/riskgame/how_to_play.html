How to play

Points & Goal Zero

Earn points by producing resources. Each resource is worth 100 points. Points are multiplied by Goal Zero, which equals the number of turns you've played without an incident happening.

Game Duration

The game lasts two weeks and is broken down into two episodes. Each episode consists of seven turns. Each turn lasts 24 hours.

Player Roles

Your team consists of office players and frontline players. These roles determine what a player can do.

Actions

Your team has a shared pool of actions for office players equal to four times the number of office players in the team, and a shared pool of actions for frontline players equal to two times the number of office players in the team. Players can use these actions to do things. There is no limit to the number of actions they can spend. At the start of each turn, these actions are replenished.

Office Player

The office player has her own station, which has a unique safety and production quality profile. Office players can do the following things:

  • Inspect production quality
  • Improve production quality
  • Inspect safety
  • Improve safety
  • Place barriers
  • Plan production
  • Produce resources

All these things cost one action, except resource production, which is free.

Frontline Player

The frontline player does not have her own station but can do the following things to support the office players in her team:

  • Inspect an office player's safety
  • Predict an office player's event for the next turn

All these things cost one action.

Events

At the start of each turn, office players may receive an event. Events modify certain aspects of the game, either for you or for your whole team. Note that frontline players can see what the an office player's event for the next turn will be.

Managing Your Station

As an office player, there are two important aspects of the game you must manage: your station's safety, and its production quality.

Managing Safety

Your station's safety is represented by a stack of eight tokens. Each token is either blank or a risk. To get a partial view of your stack's composition, you can inspect safety. To decrease the number of risks in your safety stack, you can improve safety.

Improving Safety

Each time you improve safety, a blank token is added to your safety stack, it is shuffled and a random token is removed.

Improving Production Quality

Each time you improve production quality, a resource token is added to your production quality stack, it is shuffled and a random token is removed.

Planning Production & Producing Resources

To produce resources, you must reserve at least one resource production slot and may optionally place barriers. For each reserved resource production slot, one token will be drawn from your production quality stack and from your safety stack. An additional token is drawn for each resource encountered as well as for each risk, until only blanks are drawn or a stack is empty.

Barriers

Each placed barrier prevents one risk from turning into an incident. Barriers are lost after resource production regardless of any risks turning up.

Incidents

You score points for your resources as long as no incident happens. If an incident does happen, you do not score any points and your goal zero is reset.

Wear

After resource production, your station's safety and production quality experience wear. A risk token is added to the former and a blank token is added to the latter. Both stacks are shuffled and a random token is removed from each.
